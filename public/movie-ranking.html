Best movies according to me

My top 3 movies of all time

  1. The Shawshank Redemption

    "The Shawshank Redemption" is a 1994 drama film about Andy Dufresne, a banker wrongly convicted of murder and sentenced to life in Shawshank State Penitentiary. In prison, he befriends Red and maintains hope while enduring the harsh realities. Andy secretly plans his escape, using his skills and connections to improve the lives of fellow inmates. After years of perseverance, he successfully escapes and starts a new life in Mexico. Red, inspired by Andy's resilience, follows him, and they reunite on a beach, symbolizing their triumph over adversity. The film explores themes of hope, friendship, and the human spirit's resilience.

  2. Parasite

    "Parasite" is a 2019 South Korean film directed by Bong Joon-ho. The story follows the impoverished Kim family, who cunningly infiltrate the lives of the wealthy Park family by securing employment with them. The Kims exploit their positions, gradually taking advantage of the Parks' trust and wealth. However, their carefully constructed scheme unravels when a hidden secret threatens to expose them. The film delves into themes of class inequality, social hierarchy, and the consequences of greed. With a blend of dark humor and suspense, "Parasite" provides a thought-provoking commentary on the disparities and complexities of modern society.

  3. Shutter Island

    "Shutter Island" is a 2010 psychological thriller film directed by Martin Scorsese. Set in 1954, the story revolves around U.S. Marshal Teddy Daniels, who is sent to investigate the disappearance of a patient at the Ashecliffe Hospital for the criminally insane on Shutter Island. As Teddy delves deeper into the investigation, he uncovers disturbing secrets and experiences strange occurrences, leading him to question his own sanity. The film explores themes of memory, identity, and the nature of reality, keeping the audience on the edge of their seats until the shocking truth is revealed, challenging everything Teddy thought he knew.
